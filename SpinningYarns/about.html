About Spinning Yarns

Mission Statement

Spinning Yarns is a collective of feminist electronic literature creators who use technology and textiles to push boundaries and challenge norms. Our work explores the intersection of text, art, and activism, and we strive to amplify marginalized voices and perspectives.

Join us in the fight for a more just and equitable world.

PixelGrrl

PixelGrrl is a member of Spinning Yarns and an electronic literature creator with a passion for literary surrealism and glitch art. She has been an active member of the collective since the early 90s, contributing to a variety of projects that explore the intersection of text, technology, and textile.

PixelGrrl's work often incorporates elements of literary surrealism, drawing on techniques like automatic writing and stream-of-consciousness narration to create dreamlike and often unsettling narratives. She is particularly interested in exploring the ways in which digital technology can be used to augment and subvert traditional forms of storytelling, and her work often includes interactive elements that allow readers to engage with her texts in unconventional ways.

PixelGrrl is also an accomplished glitch artist, using digital manipulation techniques to create works that distort and remix images in unexpected ways. She has experimented with glitched textile art as well, using computer code to create patterns and textures that are then woven into fabric.

In addition to her work with Spinning Yarns, PixelGrrl is an active member of the literary surrealism community, and has published several works in journals dedicated to the genre. She also frequently collaborates with other glitch artists and hackers to create new works that push the boundaries of what is possible with digital technology.
